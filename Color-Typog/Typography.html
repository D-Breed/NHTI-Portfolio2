Typography - Styling in the Modern World

Creating a style for a website or printed material, such as a brochure, poster,
or menu, involves many elements, altogether called Typography.


Typography is about making digital or printed materials functional for their purpose.
Function means it is readable, clear, emotionally impactful as related to the purpose,
and assists the reader in focusing. How we focus can vary greatly when reading a menu,
a novel, or finding information on a website. The bottom line is overall effectiveness
in delivering a story, a message, or just the facts.

Design elements

Typography is about many elements:

  • Fonts: Clean, legible, and helpful to comprehension.
  • Hierarchy: Titles and Headings to guide a reader
  • Spacing of Text: Adequate between lines, between letters, in margins to avoid clutter
  • Alignment of Elements:Consistent to create structure and professionalism
  • Layout Design:
    • Contrast: between text and backgrounds
    • Repeating: consistent design approach, helping the reader to find a flow
    • Whitespace: can help create focus, separate, and enhance readability
    • Cultural: like color, fonts can also have an emotional reaction in some cases
    • Dynamic: A great design on the big screen may flow differently on mobile

Creating great output means considering all these typography elements for the project and your target audience.
